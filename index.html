It wasn’t always called Amsterdam

Amsterdam is the Dutch capital’s modern moniker, but it was originally called Amstrelredam. The name is thought to date back as far as 1275 and refers to the dam built across the Amstel River to prevent flooding. As Amsterdam grew, its name was shortened and simplified, and Amsterdam became the norm.
