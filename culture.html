In the Netherlands, there’s a long tradition of “gezelligheid” — a word with no perfect English translation. It describes a cosy, warm feeling of togetherness — like sharing good company, laughter, and comfort (whether in a café, at home, or on a canal boat).

As for medieval culture, one fun fact is that medieval festivals often featured “fool’s days,” where peasants could mock nobles, wear crowns, and play pranks — a rare time when social rules were flipped upside down for laughter and freedom!
